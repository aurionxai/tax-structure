When Trading Starts,
The Clock Starts.

Some things must be done before the first trade. Some within 75 days. Some by year-end. Miss the window — the benefit is gone forever.

BEFORE FIRST TRADE — miss this, it's gone
WITHIN 75 DAYS — hard legal deadline
WITHIN YEAR ONE — do it or leave money behind
EVERY YEAR — ongoing, forever
If You Get These Wrong, Nothing Else Matters
  • Trading in a personal account before the LP exists = those trades are permanently outside §475. You cannot go back.
  • §475(f) election missed by even one day = wash-sale exposure re-enters; ordinary loss cap returns. No cure.
  • S-corp Form 2553 missed = entity taxed as C-corp or default LLC for the entire year. Retroactive fixes exist but are expensive and not guaranteed.
  • PPLI funded before investor-control structure is verified = retroactive ordinary income + penalties on the entire policy value.
  • Cash-balance plan not adopted by December 31 = zero CB contribution allowed for that year. Age-based catch-up gone.
Phase 0
Before the First Trade — No Exceptions
Form the Trading LP and all four S-corps
Day 0
Capital lives in the LP. Trading happens in the LP. Brokerage account is in the LP's name. Personal accounts are for personal money — not trading the same instruments. No exceptions after this point.
§475(f) Internal Resolution — Adopt BEFORE First Trade
Day 0
The partnership adopts a written internal resolution electing §475(f) mark-to-market. Dated before or on the day of the first trade. No IRS filing required — just the resolution in your records. This is what eliminates wash-sale exposure and uncaps ordinary losses. If trading begins without this: those trades are outside §475 permanently. The attorney drafts this on formation day. Do not trade first and ask questions later.
Open brokerage accounts in the LP's name — not personal names
Day 0
The LP is the trader. The LP holds the capital. The LP gets the K-1s. Trading in a personal account while waiting for the LLC/LP docs to arrive means those trades are taxed at your individual rate with no §475 protection.
Freeze personal brokerage accounts in substantially identical securities
Day 0
§475 kills wash sales inside the LP. But if a partner's personal Schwab or Fidelity account holds the same tickers, cross-account wash sale rules can still fire. Stop trading the same names personally the day LP trading begins. Close the positions or leave them alone.
Phase 1
Within 75 Days of Entity Formation — Hard Deadline
S-corp Election — Form 2553
Day 75 max
Each partner's management company must elect S-corp status within 75 days of formation (or by March 15 of the first full tax year). Miss this = C-corp or default LLC all year. The attorney files Form 2553. Get the IRS acceptance letter in writing. Do not assume it was accepted.
Confirm §475 election on first Form 3115 or tax return disclosure
Year 1 return
The internal resolution is the election. It is confirmed formally on the first partnership tax return (Form 1065) or by attaching a disclosure statement. The CPA handles this at filing — but they need to know the election was made and have the dated resolution in the file.
§754 Election in the LP Agreement
Day 1 of LP
This must be in the partnership agreement from the start or elected on the first partnership return. It allows future partners (or heirs) to step up inside basis when they acquire an interest. Retrofitting it after the fact is complex. The attorney adds it to the LP agreement on day one — zero incremental cost, large future benefit.
Management fee language in LP Agreement — guaranteed payments only
Before first fee
Each partner's S-corp charges the LP a management fee. The LP agreement must specify this is a guaranteed payment under §707(c) or a partner-specific capital account charge — not a general expense. If it's a general expense, all partners share the cost. The attorney must draft this correctly before any fee is paid.
Set S-corp W-2 salary — sized to CB plan contribution
Before first payroll
The W-2 must be at least as large as the planned cash-balance contribution. Age 40 partner wanting a $100K CB contribution needs at least $100K W-2. Age 55 partner wanting $220K needs at least $220K W-2. Set payroll from the first check. You cannot retroactively fix an underpaid W-2.
Build year-one cash flow model with CPA
Before Q1 ends
The number that surprises everyone: ~$1.25M per partner per quarter in estimated taxes (Form 1040-ES). No LP withholding. Four partners paying $5M/quarter while simultaneously funding PPLI premiums, CB plans, and O&G is a serious liquidity planning problem. Build this model before the first quarter ends. Underpayment penalty is 8%+ — don't pay it.

Phase 2
Within Year One — Do It or Leave It Behind
PPLI medical underwriting — start immediately, takes 60–90 days
ASAP — Q1
Medical underwriting for PPLI takes 60–90 days minimum. Start it the same week entities are formed — not after. Any health event between now and the exam can change terms or bar coverage entirely. Schedule disability insurance exams at the same time; they use the same medical data. This is the most time-sensitive item after §475.
PPLI investor-control structure verified and documented
Before first premium
Before one dollar enters the PPLI policy: the IDF manager must be independent, with their own systems and capability to serve other clients. The strategy mandate must be a written document — not a phone call. The attorney must provide a written opinion that the investor-control doctrine is satisfied. Fund the policy only after this is in writing.
Cash-balance / defined-benefit plan adopted
By Dec 31, Year 1
The plan document must be adopted by December 31 to make deductible contributions for that tax year. Contributions can be made up to the business tax return due date (with extensions), but the plan must exist by year-end. An actuary sizes the contribution per partner based on age and W-2. This is done through the S-corp, not the LP.
Solo 401(k) established and funded
By Dec 31, Year 1
Same rule as the CB plan — plan must exist by December 31. Elective deferrals and employer contributions are made through the S-corp payroll. Set up the plan before year-end and fund it when cash is available (by return due date with extension).
Annual gift exclusion — $19K/person, use it before Dec 31
By Dec 31
Each partner can give $19K/yr per recipient ($38K if gifting with spouse) with zero tax, zero reporting, zero structure. Four kids = $76–152K moved outside the estate per partner, per year. You cannot roll unused exclusion into next year. Write the checks in Q4 if you haven't already.
529 superfunding — $90K/child, 5-year election, year one only
Year 1 (optional)
Each partner can front-load 5 years of annual gift exclusions into a 529 plan: $90K/child ($180K with spouse gifting). One-time election. Immediately removes that amount from the taxable estate. Each child gets their own 529. Unused funds convert to the child's Roth IRA after 15 years.
SLAT / IDGT executed — lock in the $15M exemption now
Year 1 — do not wait
The estate and gift tax exemption is currently ~$15M/person. Future legislation could reduce it. A SLAT or IDGT executed now locks in the current exemption permanently — even if the law changes later. Each year of delay is a year of potential appreciation that stays in the taxable estate. The attorney needs 4–8 weeks to draft. Start immediately. Note: SLAT is irrevocable. Each partner must understand the divorce risk before signing.
South Dakota dynasty trust established — for PPLI and long-term assets
Year 1
The SD trust holds the PPLI policies (to keep death benefit outside the estate) and receives gifted LP interests. Requires a South Dakota corporate trustee. No rule against perpetuities — wealth moves to grandchildren and beyond with no forced distribution. Get the trust in place before the PPLI policy is issued so the trust can be the owner from inception.
Year-end MTM valuation policy — written before December 31
Before Dec 31, Year 1
Under §475, every position is marked to FMV on December 31. The CPA needs a written valuation methodology before the first year-end close — especially for any less-liquid options where market data is thin. The IRS can challenge year-end marks. A written policy with independent price verification is the defense. Draft it before the year ends, not after.
O&G working interests — deploy Q3/Q4 to capture year-one IDC deduction
Q3–Q4, Year 1
Intangible Drilling Costs (IDCs) are deductible in the year incurred — but only if the well is actually drilled that year. For a year-one deduction, the investment must be in and the well spudded before December 31. Source the O&G program by Q2; fund by Q3 at the latest. Confirm direct-ownership structure (not LLC) before deploying any capital.
Disability insurance — own-occupation policy, issued before any health event
ASAP — Week 1
An active HFT partner's income stops the day they can't work. Own-occupation individual disability insurance replaces income if they become unable to perform their specific occupation. Get underwriting started immediately — it uses the same medical exam as PPLI underwriting. BOE (Business Overhead Expense) insurance covers firm fixed costs during disability. A single health event can make this unavailable or unaffordable.
Phase 3
Every Year, Forever — The Maintenance Stack
Quarterly estimated taxes — $1.25M/partner, four times a year
Apr · Jun · Sep · Jan
Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15. No exceptions. The LP has no withholding. The CPA builds a quarterly true-up model to adjust each payment as income is known. Missing or underpaying = 8%+ IRS penalty on the shortfall. Treat this like a payroll obligation.
§817(h) diversification test — every quarter-end, IDF manager
Mar · Jun · Sep · Dec
The IDF manager must confirm compliance at March 31, June 30, September 30, and December 31. If a concentrated position would fail the test, it must be reduced before quarter-end. The carrier should receive a written compliance certification each quarter. This is not optional — a failed quarter can jeopardize the entire policy's tax-free status.
PPLI 7-Pay Test monitoring — every premium, every benefit change
Every premium
Every premium payment must pass the 7-Pay Test or the policy becomes a Modified Endowment Contract. MEC = policy loans are taxable income + 10% penalty. The carrier confirms compliance each time — build this into the payment process. Any increase in death benefit also resets the test.
TTS documentation — each partner, contemporaneous, all year
Daily
Each partner maintains their own log: trades reviewed, risk management decisions made, research hours, market analysis. IRS challenges TTS at the individual return level — not the LP level. A partner who can't show 750+ hours of substantial, frequent, regular trading activity is at risk. The log doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist.
Annual compliance calendar — attorney + CPA review
Annually — Q1
Form 1065 (Mar 15), 4× Form 1120-S (Mar 15), 4× individual returns (Apr 15 / Oct 15), Form 5500 CB plan (Jul 31), Form 709 gift tax for any transfers (Apr 15), §817(h) quarterly certs, TTS substantiation, nexus/domicile review, reasonable compensation update, valuation appraisals for any gifts, PPLI loan-to-value review.
Annual gift exclusion — $19K/person, use before Dec 31
By Dec 31
Every year. Does not roll over. Takes 10 minutes per partner. Four partners gifting to four kids with both spouses gifting = $304K/yr permanently out of the estate. Over 10 years that is $3M+ moved tax-free with no structure and no professional fees.
Reasonable compensation review — each S-corp, annually
Annually
The IRS requires S-corp owner-employees to pay themselves reasonable compensation before taking distributions. As the management fee income grows, the W-2 must also remain reasonable relative to the services rendered and industry benchmarks. Document it with a comp study each year. An underpaid W-2 is an automatic audit flag.
The One Rule
The structure only protects the trades that happen inside it. Every day you trade before the LP exists, every election you miss, every quarter you don't document — that money is gone to taxes permanently. Build it first. Then trade.
