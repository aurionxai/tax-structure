Two Buckets

Flat 🪨

Fixed costs — stays ~$200–370K/yr

CPA team, attorney, retirement plan admin, disability insurance, valuations, family office coordination. Doesn't change much as wealth grows. This is the boring baseline cost of running everything properly.

Grows 🌊

PPLI management fee — grows with your money

About 1.25% of your policy assets per year. Year 1: small. Year 5: bigger. Year 10: large — but so is the tax-free pool it's managing. Paying 1.25% to avoid 40.8% is the best trade available.

Note 💸

Most of this is deductible

CPA fees, attorney fees, family office costs, retirement plan admin — all deductible through your S-corp as ordinary business expenses. The after-tax net cost is ~40% lower than the gross numbers above. Real cost: ~$180K (Year 1) → ~$600K (Year 5+).

The Expensive Mistake

Never 🪤

Don't shop on price

The CPA who charges $30K instead of $80K but misses the 475 election deadline just cost you $5M+ in wash-sale phantom income. The attorney who charges $20K instead of $100K but misses the PPLI investor-control doctrine loses the policy entirely. You get what you pay for — and in this structure, you pay for it once and the mistake is permanent.

Never 🔥

Don't hire generalists

You need a trader-tax CPA — not a general practice CPA. TTS qualification, §475 elections, and mark-to-market accounting are specialties. Most CPAs have never filed a §475 election. Most estate attorneys have never structured a PPLI. Specialist cost: 2–3× general CPA. Specialist value: 100×.

Good news ✓

You said attorney-managed

This structure is being managed by an attorney and staff. That's the right call. The attorney coordinates the CPA, the PPLI specialist, the TPA, and the trustees — one quarterback, no gaps. The cost numbers above assume exactly this model. No one falls through the cracks.

The Number That Matters 🪨

You spend ~2–3% of your income to protect it. You keep ~5× what you spend in saved taxes — every year, growing. By year 10, the structure saves more per year than most people earn in a lifetime. The cost is not the question. The question is whether the professionals execute it correctly. That's why the attorney leads.
