The Seven Moves

Foundation 🏛️

1 · Two companies per partner

Company One trades (shared). Zero employees. K-1 flows to each partner — no W-2, no payroll tax on trading gains. Company Two = each partner's own solo S-corp. Fully independent. Each partner owns it, runs it, is responsible for it.

Not optional 📜

2 · File the magic paper ("475")

Fast single-stock options + the dumb "wash sale" rule = owing tax on money never made. Magic paper kills the rule. Losses always count. Single-stock pays full ~40% rate regardless — paper protects, doesn't reduce rate.

⏰ 75 days after company born. Miss = wait whole year.

W-2 rule 💼

3 · Pay yourself from YOUR S-corp

Your solo S-corp must pay you a real W-2 (~$150–200K min) before distributions. All-distribution = IRS audit bait. Rest = distributions, no payroll tax. K-1 from Company One? No salary needed. Must document real services — research, risk, admin. Thin paper = #1 solo audit trigger.

Solopreneur edge 🦴

4 · Max YOUR retirement cave

Solo S-corp = pension sized just for YOU. At age 55: ~$308K/yr total shelter per partner. Cash-balance pension (~$220K pre-tax) + 401(k) + mega backdoor Roth (~$49K → tax-free forever, 2026 limit) + backdoor Roth IRA ($8K). Older = bigger pension. Each partner independent.

Kids 👶

5 · Kids' Roth IRAs

Employ kids for real work through Company Two. Pay up to ~$15K (zero tax owed). Parent writes the $7K Roth check — IRS doesn't care who writes it, only that kid earned it. 50–60 years of tax-free compounding.

Main weapon 🛡️

6 · The big shield (PPLI)

Special life insurance wrapper. Money inside grows tax-free. Borrow out tax-free. Kids inherit tax-free. One law: you can't drive the trades inside it — independent manager must. Break that, lose everything.

Legacy 🗿

7 · Pass it down smart

Family trusts own the shield → wealth skips the 40% death tax. Charity trusts = big deductions now. Every $100M moved ≈ $40M saved later.

Truth ⚖️

The honest part

Single-stock options pay the full ~40% rate. No legal trick changes that character. Only index options (SPX-kind) get ~27% — move bets there only if it doesn't hurt the edge. Never trade alpha for tax.

🔥

Touch These? Fire. Bad.

Tribes that touched these lost everything:

  • Offshore funds — does nothing for US citizens. IRS sees all. Always.
  • Captive insurance — on the IRS hit list. People lose in court constantly.
  • C-corp "21% trick" — hidden extra taxes make it worse, not better.
  • "Turn fast money into slow money" tricks — famous fund tried. Paid $7,000,000,000 to settle. Biggest tax settlement ever.

Easy Wins — Day One

Available the moment the S-corps are formed. Low effort, real money, fully legal.

Free money 🏠

Augusta Rule (§280A)

Rent your home to your S-corp for up to 14 days/yr for business meetings. Rental income = tax-free to you. S-corp deducts it. Nice home @ $1,500/day × 10 meetings = $15K/yr free. Needs genuine meetings + docs.

Immediate 🏥

Health Insurance + HSA

Family health premiums 100% deductible through S-corp. Add a High-Deductible plan → fund an HSA: $8,550/family (2026), triple tax advantage — deduct in, grow tax-free, spend tax-free. After 65 = second IRA.

No-brainer 📋

Accountable Plan

S-corp reimburses: home office, phone, internet, data feeds, Bloomberg, equipment, professional dev. Not income to you. Deductible to S-corp. Easy $10–30K/yr per partner with zero extra tax owed.

Year-one 🚙

Heavy Vehicle (§179)

Business-use vehicle over 6,000 lbs GVWR → full §179 or 100% bonus depreciation year one. $80–120K SUV = $33–49K tax saved in year one. Must be genuine business use and documented.

Quick 👶

Dependent Care FSA + §127

Pre-tax $5K/yr childcare through S-corp FSA. Stack with §127 education assistance: S-corp pays $5,250/yr in tuition or student loan payments — deductible by corp, not income to you.

Long game 🚀

QSBS §1202 (Angel Investing)

Invest in early-stage C-corps alongside the trading op. Hold 5+ years → up to $10M in gains excluded from tax entirely per investment. The only legal zero-tax exit for US citizens. Stack across multiple investments.

🏰

Family Wealth Office

At $50M+/yr income, a Single Family Office (SFO) transitions from optional to institutional-grade infrastructure. It pays for itself.

Key lever 🧾

Everything becomes deductible

TCJA killed investment-expense deductions for individuals. A properly structured family office operating as a genuine business deducts ALL operating costs — staff, tech, legal, accounting, office. Lender Management LLC v. Commissioner confirmed this. Full-time CFO + CPA + attorney = deductible.

SEC exempt 🛡️

No RIA registration needed

Dodd-Frank Rule 202(a)(11)(G)-1: a family office managing money only for family clients is exempt from SEC investment adviser registration entirely. No Form ADV, no SEC exams, no compliance overhead.

Command center 🗂️

One roof, everything coordinated

Trading LP + each S-corp + PPLI policies + dynasty trusts + real estate + charitable vehicles all under one tax strategy. Without it: 4 CPAs who don't talk. With it: one P&L, one plan, one estate strategy that actually compounds.

Access 🔑

Institutional investment access

Family offices get into PE/VC co-investments, direct deals, club deals, hedge fund side-pockets not available to individuals. At your accumulation rate, AUM qualifies for top-tier allocation within 2–3 years. QSBS stacks on top.

Math 📐

Cost vs. return

Lean SFO (2–3 staff, basic infra): $500K–$1M/yr to run — fully deductible. At 40.8% effective rate, net after-tax cost = $300–600K. Against $50M income and the Lender Management deduction stack, it pays for itself year one.

Legacy 🌳

Multi-generational by design

The family office owns or coordinates dynasty trusts, PPLI policies, kids' Roth IRAs, and QSBS investments. Wealth transfer becomes systematic, not reactive. Built once, runs for generations.

One Rule Above All 🪵

Don't do this alone. Hire trader-tax CPA + tax lawyer. Deadlines bite hard and paperwork must be real. Big firms have no secret magic — they do this exact boring stuff, perfectly, every year. That ~18–20% floor? It's where legal ends. Everything below it has a court case attached.
