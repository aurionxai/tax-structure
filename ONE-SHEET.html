The Eight Moves

Foundation 🏛️

1 · Three entities, built together

Company One trades (shared LP). Zero employees. K-1 flows to each partner — no W-2, no payroll tax on trading gains. Company Two = each partner's own solo S-corp. Fully independent. Each partner owns theirs 100%. Company Three = creator's IP LLC. Owns the software. Not shared. Not the LP's. See Move 8.

Not optional 📜

2 · File the magic paper ("475")

Fast single-stock options + the dumb "wash sale" rule = owing tax on money never made. Magic paper kills the rule. Losses always count. Single-stock pays full ~40% rate regardless — paper protects, doesn't reduce rate.

⏰ 75 days after company born. Miss = wait whole year.

W-2 rule 💼

3 · Pay yourself from YOUR S-corp

Your solo S-corp must pay you a real W-2 (~$150–200K min) before distributions. All-distribution = IRS audit bait. Rest = distributions, no payroll tax. K-1 from Company One? No salary needed. Must document real services — research, risk, admin. Thin paper = #1 solo audit trigger.

Solopreneur edge 🦴

4 · Max YOUR retirement cave

Solo S-corp = pension sized just for YOU. At age 55: ~$308K/yr total shelter per partner. Cash-balance pension (~$220K pre-tax) + 401(k) + mega backdoor Roth (~$49K → tax-free forever, 2026 limit) + backdoor Roth IRA ($8K). Older = bigger pension. Each partner independent.

Kids 👶

5 · Kids' Roth IRAs

Employ kids for real work through Company Two. Pay up to ~$15K (zero tax owed). Parent writes the $7K Roth check — IRS doesn't care who writes it, only that kid earned it. 50–60 years of tax-free compounding.

Main weapon 🛡️

6 · The big shield (PPLI)

Special life insurance wrapper. Money inside grows tax-free. Borrow out tax-free. Kids inherit tax-free. One law: you can't drive the trades inside it — independent manager must. Break that, lose everything.

Legacy 🗿

7 · Pass it down smart

Family trusts own the shield → wealth skips the 40% death tax. Charity trusts = big deductions now. Every $100M moved ≈ $40M saved later.

Creator only ⚙️

8 · The software lives in its own cave

The algorithm is not owned by the Trading LP. It belongs to the creator — in their own IP LLC. The LP licenses it under a written deal and pays a royalty. Revenue split ≠ IP ownership. If partners split, creator keeps the software. If the LP folds, creator keeps the software. Must be assigned to the IP LLC before the first trade — waiting until after means assigning appreciated IP, which triggers a tax bill.

Truth ⚖️

The honest part

Single-stock options pay the full ~40% rate. No legal trick changes that character. Only index options (SPX-kind) get ~27% — move bets there only if it doesn't hurt the edge. Never trade alpha for tax.

Touch These? Fire. Bad.

Tribes that touched these lost everything:

  • Offshore funds — does nothing for US citizens. IRS sees all. Always.
  • Captive insurance — on the IRS hit list. People lose in court constantly.
  • C-corp "21% trick" — hidden extra taxes make it worse, not better.
  • "Turn fast money into slow money" tricks — famous fund tried. Paid $7,000,000,000 to settle. Biggest tax settlement ever.

Easy Wins — Day One

Available the moment the S-corps are formed. Low effort, real money, fully legal.

Free money 🏠

Augusta Rule (§280A)

Rent your home to your S-corp for up to 14 days/yr for business meetings. Rental income = tax-free to you. S-corp deducts it. Nice home @ $1,500/day × 10 meetings = $15K/yr free. Needs genuine meetings + docs.

Immediate 🏥

Health Insurance + HSA

Family health premiums 100% deductible through S-corp. Add a High-Deductible plan → fund an HSA: $8,550/family (2026), triple tax advantage — deduct in, grow tax-free, spend tax-free. After 65 = second IRA.

No-brainer 📋

Accountable Plan

S-corp reimburses: home office, phone, internet, data feeds, Bloomberg, equipment, professional dev. Not income to you. Deductible to S-corp. Easy $10–30K/yr per partner with zero extra tax owed.

Year-one 🚙

Heavy Vehicle (§179)

Business-use vehicle over 6,000 lbs GVWR → full §179 or 100% bonus depreciation year one. $80–120K SUV = $33–49K tax saved in year one. Must be genuine business use and documented.

Quick 👶

Dependent Care FSA + §127

Pre-tax $5K/yr childcare through S-corp FSA. Stack with §127 education assistance: S-corp pays $5,250/yr in tuition or student loan payments — deductible by corp, not income to you.

Long game 🚀

QSBS §1202 (Angel Investing)

Invest in early-stage C-corps alongside the trading op. Hold 5+ years → up to $10M in gains excluded from tax entirely per investment. The only legal zero-tax exit for US citizens. Stack across multiple investments.

Family Wealth Office

At $50M+/yr income, a Single Family Office (SFO) transitions from optional to institutional-grade infrastructure. It pays for itself.

Key lever 🧾

Everything becomes deductible

TCJA killed investment-expense deductions for individuals. A properly structured family office operating as a genuine business deducts ALL operating costs — staff, tech, legal, accounting, office. Lender Management LLC v. Commissioner confirmed this. Full-time CFO + CPA + attorney = deductible.

SEC exempt 🛡️

No RIA registration needed

Dodd-Frank Rule 202(a)(11)(G)-1: a family office managing money only for family clients is exempt from SEC investment adviser registration entirely. No Form ADV, no SEC exams, no compliance overhead.

Command center 🗂️

One roof, everything coordinated

Trading LP + each S-corp + PPLI policies + dynasty trusts + real estate + charitable vehicles all under one tax strategy. Without it: 4 CPAs who don't talk. With it: one P&L, one plan, one estate strategy that actually compounds.

Access 🔑

Institutional investment access

Family offices get into PE/VC co-investments, direct deals, club deals, hedge fund side-pockets not available to individuals. At your accumulation rate, AUM qualifies for top-tier allocation within 2–3 years. QSBS stacks on top.

Math 📐

Cost vs. return

Lean SFO (2–3 staff, basic infra): $500K–$1M/yr to run — fully deductible. At 40.8% effective rate, net after-tax cost = $300–600K. Against $50M income and the Lender Management deduction stack, it pays for itself year one.

Legacy 🌳

Multi-generational by design

The family office owns or coordinates dynasty trusts, PPLI policies, kids' Roth IRAs, and QSBS investments. Wealth transfer becomes systematic, not reactive. Built once, runs for generations.

Protect, Transfer & Shield

Gaps most advisors miss. Attorney executes these. You need to know they exist — and most are time-sensitive.

Free. Now. 🎁

Annual gifts — $19K/person/yr

Give $19,000/yr to each family member — zero gift tax, no return needed. With spouse: $38K/person/yr. Four kids per partner = $152K+ transferred silently every year. No structure, no entity, no planning lag. Start the day you form the companies.

Kids 🎓

529 Superfunding

Front-load $90K per child in one shot (5-year election — counts as 5 years of gifts at once). With spouse: $180K/child, year one. SECURE 2.0 bonus: after 15 years, roll unused funds into the kid's Roth IRA ($35K lifetime). Education + retirement in one vehicle. Start the 15-year clock now.

Act now ⏳

Lock in the $15M exemption (SLAT / IDGT)

You can give or move $15M/person out of your estate right now with zero gift tax. This number may drop to ~$7M in future law. Window is open. Do it now. SLAT: irrevocable trust for spouse — moves assets out of your estate today while spouse can still benefit. IDGT: sell LP interests to a trust on a note — future gains leave your estate immediately. Attorney executes. Don't wait.

Free leverage 📉

Valuation discounts on LP gifts

When you gift a minority interest in the Trading LP, an appraiser applies a 20–40% discount for lack of control + lack of marketability. Your $15M exemption effectively moves $23M of economic value out of your estate. Requires a qualified appraisal filed with the gift return. Attorney coordinates.

Best laws 🏔️

South Dakota dynasty trusts

Dynasty trust goes in South Dakota — not Wyoming or Delaware. Why: no Rule Against Perpetuities (trust runs forever, not 1,000 years), strongest asset protection laws in the US, no SD state income tax on trust earnings. Needs an SD corporate trustee. The SD trust owns your PPLI policies — wealth compounds forever, passes income-tax-free + estate-tax-free.

Creditor shield ⚔️

Wyoming LLC charging order

A creditor who wins a judgment against one partner cannot force a sale of the Trading LP. Wyoming law: they can only receive a "charging order" — share of distributions if any are declared. In practice: you never declare one, they never collect. Wyoming's protection is the strongest in the US. Pair with a South Dakota DAPT for layered individual protection.

Critical gap 🏥

Disability insurance — own-occupation

Your most valuable asset is your ability to trade. If you can't trade, the K-1 disappears. Own-occupation disability insurance replaces income specifically if you can't do your job. PPLI builds slowly — it doesn't protect you in year one. Deductible through S-corp. Do the medical exam at the same time as PPLI underwriting — one trip, two critical policies. Most time-sensitive item on this page.

The PPLI isn't just a tax tool — it's a compounding machine that eventually makes the taxable trading operation optional. Here's the full picture.

How fast can you fund it?

The IRS 7-Pay Test limits annual premiums relative to the death benefit. Bigger death benefit = more funding room per year. 4 partners = 4 independent policies = 4× the runway.

How you take the money out

Primary 🏦

Policy loans (tax-free)

Borrow against the cash value. Not income — it's a loan. Policy still earns full returns on the entire balance including the loaned amount. Interest accrues ~1–3%/yr. At death: death benefit repays the loan, surplus passes tax-free to trust. You live on loans forever.

Ultimate exit 🎯

Death benefit (best outcome)

Paid to beneficiaries income-tax-free (§101a). If dynasty trust owns the policy → estate-tax-free too. The entire compounded pool — decades of 50%+ tax-free returns — transfers to heirs with zero tax event. Ever.

Never 🚫

Surrender (never do this)

Full surrender = all gain hits as ordinary income immediately. Wipes out decades of tax-free compounding in one event. The only scenario worse: let the policy lapse with loans outstanding — same catastrophic income hit plus penalties. Manage loan balance ≤ 75–80% of cash value.

Borrow · Borrow · Die → The sequence
